## Overview

For your final project, you will work in **groups of 2 (or 3) students** to explore an Artificial Intelligence or Machine Learning topic in greater depth.

The goal of this project is to:
- **Learn a topic more deeply**
- Become a **“mini-expert”** (or at a minimum, significantly expand your understanding)
- **Teach the topic to the class**

Your group will design and deliver a **20–30 minute lesson**, along with supporting materials and an **activity or assignment** that helps others learn the concept.

---

## Topic Selection

Choose:
- A topic **not covered in depth** in class, OR  
- A topic from class that you **expand significantly**

*Instructor approval required.*


## Team Expectations

- All group members must contribute meaningfully  
- Groups of **2–3 students only**
- Be prepared to answer questions about any part of your project

---
## Project Components

---
### 1. Lecture & Teaching Materials (75 points)

Develop a **20–30 minute class lesson**.

Include:
- Handout and/or slides (or Markdown notes)
- Clear explanations of key concepts
- Examples and/or visuals
- Real-world applications
- 1–2 discussion or engagement questions

Your goal is to **teach the class clearly and effectively**.

---
### 2. Class Activity or Course Assignment (50 points)

Create an activity or assignment that reinforces your topic.

You may choose one of the following:
- **In-class activity** (to be completed during class time), OR  
- **Course assignment** (to be completed outside of class)

Examples:
- Short coding exercise (Python preferred)
- Guided worksheet
- Problem set
- Interactive demo
- Small group discussion activity

Must include:
- Clear instructions
- Expected outcomes or answers
- Estimated time:
  - **30–60 minutes (in-class activity)** OR  
  - **60–120 minutes (course assignment)**

Your goal is to create a learning experience that helps students better understand and apply your topic.

---
### 3. Supporting Document (25 points)

Provide a short written overview (approx. 800–1200 words):

- Explanation of the topic
- Key concepts and terminology
- Summary of how your lesson teaches the topic
- At least **3–4 credible references** (APA or MLA)

You may use AI tools, but must verify accuracy.

---
### 4. Class Presentation & Demonstration (25 points)

Deliver your **20–30 minute lesson** during the final exam period or during the April 21 or April 23 class session. Depending on the number of groups, presentations may be limited to **20 minutes**, in which case you should present the highlights of your work.

Your presentation should:
- Clearly explain your topic
- Be well-organized and appropriately paced
- Engage the class
- Include and facilitate your activity (if applicable)

You are encouraged (but not required) to include a demonstration, such as:
- Code (Python or Java)
- Simulation
- Visualization
- Activity walkthrough

---
### 5. Attendance (25 points)

Attending and actively participating in all of the presentations is 20 points of your project grade.

---
### 6. Submission

Host your project in a **GitHub repository** and submit a link to your repository on the course website.

Your repository should include all project materials.

- Organize files clearly using folders (e.g., `/slides`, `/activity`, `/docs`, `/code`)
- Include a `README.md` that:
  - Describes your topic (see Supporting Document)
  - Explains how to use your materials
  - Provides instructions for running any code (if applicable)

Make sure your repository is accessible (public or shared with the instructor).
